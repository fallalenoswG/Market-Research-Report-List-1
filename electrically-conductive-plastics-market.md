<p><p>Electrically conductive plastics are composite materials that possess the ability to conduct electricity while maintaining the lightweight and versatile properties of conventional plastics. These materials are typically created by adding conductive fillers, such as carbon black, metal fibers, or conductive polymers, to standard plastics like polyethylene or polypropylene. They are used in a variety of applications, including automotive components, electronic housings, and medical devices, due to their enhanced functionality and ability to mitigate electromagnetic interference.</p><p>The Electrically Conductive Plastics Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 8% during the forecast period. <p><p>The electrically conductive plastics market includes various types, each with distinct properties. ABS (Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene) is known for its toughness and impact resistance. PS (Polystyrene) has good insulation properties, and TPU (Thermoplastic Polyurethane) is valued for its elasticity and abrasion resistance.
