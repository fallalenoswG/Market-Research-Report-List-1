<p><ul><li>High Temperature Vulcanised (HTV)</li><li>Room Temperature Vulcanised (RTV)</li><li>Liquid Silicone Rubber (LSR)</li><li>Fluorosilicone Rubber (FSR)</li><li>High Consistency Silicone Rubber (HCR)</li><li>Silicone Gels</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The automotive silicone elastomers market includes various types, each tailored for specific applications. High Temperature Vulcanised (HTV) elastomers are durable and heat-resistant, ideal for engine components. Room Temperature Vulcanised (RTV) can cure at ambient temperatures, making them versatile for sealing and bonding. Liquid Silicone Rubber (LSR) offers excellent flow properties for intricate parts. Fluorosilicone Rubber (FSR) provides chemical resistance for fuel systems. High Consistency Silicone Rubber (HCR) is used in high-stress applications, while silicone gels offer cushioning and vibration damping, enhancing performance and reliability.</p></p>
